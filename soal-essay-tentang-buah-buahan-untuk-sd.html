2. buatlah 5 buah soal essay tentang kelompok sosial


Kelompok sosial adalah kumpulan indiviu yang bersatu menjadi satu, dikarenakan memiliki tujuan yang sama untuk dicapai.

Ciri-ciri kelompok sosial:

Anggota memiliki tujuan, visi, misi yang sama. Memiliki rasa kebersamaan dan solidaritas atas keanggotaan menjadi kelompok sosia Individu yang bergabung terhadap suatu kelompok sosia, mengakui bahwa menjadi anggota kelompok tersebut. Pihak luar juga menanggap sebagai orang tersebut sebagai anggota kelompok tersebut

Syarat-syarat terbentuknya kelompok sosial:

Setiap anggota menyadari bagian dari kelompok sosial yang diikuti tersebut Kelompok sosial, memiiki norma dan perilaku sama untuk anggotanya Anggota kelompok memiiki latar belakang serta karateristik

Faktor-faktor pembentuk kelompok sosial:

Kesadaran kolektif Kesamaan karakteristik Memiliki tujuan yang sama

Klasifikasi kelompok sosial menurut Ferdinand Tonnies:

Paguyuban atau gemeinschaft adalah kumpulan indiviu yang bersatu menjadi satu karena memiliki ikatan batin yang kuat serta murni. Seperti ikatan batin dengan keluarga, sekitar tempat tinggal, pemikiran yang sama Patembayan atau gesellschaft adalah kumpulan indiviu yang bersatu karena memiliki ikatakan batin yang jangka waktu pendek, seperti perkumpulan profesi yang sama, bersifat formal, sesuai kenyataan sosial. 21. Buatlah essay dengan tema “Pentingnya Menjalin Hubungan Internasional” Di dalam essay, berikan 1 buah contoh hubungan internasional antara Indonesia dengan negara lain


Jawaban:

Pentingnya Menjalin Hubungan Internasional

Hubungan internasional merupakan hal yang sangat penting dalam dunia global saat ini. Dalam era globalisasi, setiap negara harus memiliki hubungan baik dengan negara lainnya. Hal ini dikarenakan dunia semakin terbuka dan saling terhubung satu sama lainnya, baik dalam bidang politik, ekonomi, sosial, dan budaya.

Pentingnya menjalin hubungan internasional adalah untuk mempererat kerjasama antar negara dalam berbagai bidang. Hal ini akan memberikan manfaat bagi semua negara yang terlibat, baik secara langsung maupun tidak langsung. Dalam konteks globalisasi, negara yang tidak menjalin hubungan internasional yang baik dengan negara lainnya akan ketinggalan dalam perkembangan dunia dan sulit untuk bersaing dalam kancah global.

Contoh hubungan internasional antara Indonesia dengan negara lain adalah hubungan Indonesia dengan Jepang. Hubungan antara kedua negara ini sangat penting dan strategis dalam berbagai bidang, terutama dalam bidang ekonomi. Indonesia dan Jepang memiliki hubungan ekonomi yang sangat kuat, dimana Jepang merupakan salah satu investor terbesar di Indonesia. Selain itu, kedua negara juga memiliki kerjasama dalam bidang pendidikan, kebudayaan, dan politik.

Salah satu contoh kerjasama Indonesia dan Jepang dalam bidang ekonomi adalah pembangunan infrastruktur, seperti pembangunan jalan tol, pelabuhan, dan bandara. Jepang juga memberikan bantuan dalam pembangunan proyek-proyek strategis seperti kereta cepat Jakarta-Bandung dan proyek pembangkit listrik tenaga air di Indonesia.

Jawaban:Edward Jenner: The pioneer who made vaccines.

Edward was born in 17th of  May 1749 in Berkeley, Gloucestershire, England. Since his father was the vicar of Berkeley, Edward got strong basic education. At age 21, Edward became apprenticed in surgery and anatomy, at London. When Edward returned to his hometown he eventually became a very successful family doctor and surgeon.

Edward married Catherine Kingscote in March 1788. He earned his MD from the University of St Andrews in 1792. Inoculation (Artificially inducing immunity) was already a common practice but it involved serious risks, one of which was the fear that those inoculated would then transfer the disease to those around them due to becoming carriers of the said disease. In 1721 Lady Mary Wortley Montagu had imported variolation (a method of Inoculation)to Britain after having observed it in Constantinople. While Johnnie Notions had great success with his self-devised Inoculation (and was reputed to have never lost a single patient) but his method was limited to Shetland Isles. At that time 60% of Shetland Isles’s population caught smallpox, and 20% has died due to it.

By 1768, English physician John Fewster had realized that prior infection with cowpox rendered a person immune to smallpox. In the years following 1770, atleast 5 investigators in England and Germany successfully tested in humans, a cowpox vaccine against smallpox. For example, farmer Benjamin Jesty successfully vaccinated and presumably induced immunity with cowpox in his wife and two children during the 1774 smallpox epidemic, but it was not until Edward’s work that this procedure became widely known and understood. Jenner may have been aware of Jesty's procedures and success. A similar observation was later made in France by Jacques Antoine Rabaut-Pommier in 1780. Noting the common observation that milkmaids were generally immune to smallpox, Jenner postulated that the pus in the blisters that milkmaids received from cowpox protected them from smallpox.

On 14 May 1796, Edward tested his hypothesis by inoculating and eight-year-old boy who was the son of Edward’s gardener. He scraped pus from cowpox blisters on the hands of a milkmaid who had caught cowpox from a cow whose hide now hangs on the wall of St. George’s Medical School library. Phipps was the 17th case described in Edward’s first paper on vaccination. Edward inoculated Phipps in both arms that day, subsequently giving Phipps a fever and some dizziness, but no major infection. Later, he injected Phipps with variolous material (variolation material), the routine method of immunization at that time. No disease followed. The boy was later challenged with variolous material and again showed no sign of infection.  

Edward continued his research and reported it to the Royal Society, which did not publish the initial paper. After revisions and further investigations, he published his findings on the 23 cases, including his 11 months old son Robert. Some of his conclusions were correct, some erroneous; modern microbiological and microscopic methods would make his studies easier to reproduce. The medical establishment deliberated at length over his findings before accepting them. Eventually, vaccination was accepted, and in 1840, the British government banned variolation, the use of smallpox to induce immunity, and provided vaccination using cowpox free of charge.
